Is Johann Rupert the richest man in South Africa?

January 8, 2020January 8, 2020 Matthew MakelaMatthew Makela 0 Comments

Rupert may be the best-known billionaire in South Africa. He has made a series of public appearances over the past 12 months and even ended up in a spat with the leadership of the EFF –resulting in him thinking of moving abroad. Rupert is still in Mzansi, though, and his vast fortune of $7.38 billion (R103-billion) renders him the world’s 238th-richest person.

The list is updated daily on the basis of changes in the net worth of billionaires. While Oppenheimer was the 216th richest person in the word, and Rupert was the 240, the new tally puts Rupert at 241 and Oppenheimer at 245.

The net worth of Rupert currently stands at $7.36 billion (about R105 billion), while the net worth of Oppenheimer is $7.33 billion (about R104.5 billion).

Rupert-controlled public companies worth more than R600 billion ($40 billion-plus)–including Richemont’s luxury goods holding company and Remgro’s globally competitive holding company–have been established over years, rendering a wide range of owners very affluent over the years. These include workers from the department.

Rupert has founded Rand Merchant Bank, consolidated a global multi-billion-dollar luxury goods business, redirected tobacco interests, and over the past four decades has invested in and managed numerous other commercial and social efforts.

The mega-bucks earner is part of a Stellenbosch-based highly successful investment vehicle. Since his time with Remgro, his fortune has been slowly growing, and he is also the operator of a luxury watch chain. In addition, Rupert used his money to purchase title deeds in the Western Cape for hundreds of residents.
